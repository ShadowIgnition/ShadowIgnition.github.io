I led the development of KanaQuest Version 1.01 for Nintendo Switch and PC, published by Whitethorn Games and developed in Unity. Kana Quest is an educational puzzle game that aims to teach players how to read Japanese Hiragana.

For Version 1.01, I focused on implementing various stability fixes and making quality of life and accessibility improvements. To achieve this, I made several contributions:

  • I performed major refactors to the Legacy User Interface, Tutorialization, and Gameplay Systems to fix gameplay softlocks as well as to fully intergrate controller support.
  • I performed refactors to the legacy codebase, ensuring better maintainability and stability.
  • I modularized the code, implemented error handling, and fixed incomplete logic to address edge cases.

Additionally, I introduced the following features:

  • I implemented Full Controller/Keyboard support, customizing the button prompts for different platforms such as Mouse and Keyboard, Nintendo Switch, Xbox, Playstation, and aftermarket controllers.
  • To enhance the learning experience, I developed a popup system that provides players with helpful hints and reminders regarding the game's intricacies and Kana interactions.
  • I added a dyslexic font option to improve the readability of the pixel art text.
  • Recognizing the needs of players with motion sensitivity, I included an option to reduce motion and animation of Kana characters and visual effects.

Throughout the development process, I collaborated closely with Whitethorn Games' Quality Assurance, Porting, and Accessibility Teams, ensuring a smooth, accessible and mostly bug-free experience for players on both consoles and PCs.
